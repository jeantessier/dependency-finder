More thoughts about replacing Jakarta ORO with =java.util.regex=.  If I remove
the "=/=" around regular expressions, it makes it hard to find where one
regular expression stops and the next one begins when they are in the same
string.  I cannot use "=,=" as a separator, because it shows up legitimately in
method signatures.  But what about newlines?  I don't need multi-line regular
expressions, so I could use "one expression per line" in the UI.  I would need
to use textareas instead of textfields.  Ideally, the textareas would grow and
shrink as needed.

In the backend, I can parse the regular expressions with:

    inputString.lines()
        .map(String::strip)
        .toList()

And treat the resulting lisf of strings as a list of regular expressions.
